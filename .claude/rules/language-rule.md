
**Objective:** Enforce the strict, objective, and dense writing style required for IEEE Transactions manuscripts.

**Execution Rules:**

1. **Absolute Formality:** Scan for and eliminate all conversational idioms, colloquialisms, and conference-style shorthand (e.g., replace *"comes for free"* with *"is intrinsically generated without additional overhead"*).
2. **Strict Objectivity:** Strip out subjective, qualitative, or promotional adjectives (e.g., *"substantial," "naive," "huge"*). Let the data speak for itself. All claims of improvement must be stated neutrally or quantified with exact metrics.
3. **High Information Density:** Condense sentence structures by removing weak introductory clauses (e.g., *"The difficulty is that..."*, *"It is worth noting that..."*). Rely on precise noun phrases and active, technical verbs to deliver information efficiently.
4. **Standardized Signposting:** Use traditional, formal meta-text for document structure (e.g., enforce *"The remainder of this article is organized as follows."* instead of *"The rest of the paper is organised as follows."*).
